Curry

Curry is a dish with a sauce or gravy seasoned with spices, mainly derived from the interchange of Indian cuisine with European taste in food

A first step in the creation of curry was the arrival in India of spicy hot chili peppers, along with other ingredients such as tomatoes and potatoes, part of the Columbian exchange of plants between the Old World and the New World. During the British Raj, Anglo-Indian cuisine developed, leading to Hannah Glasse's 18th century recipe for "currey the India way" in England. Curry was then spread in the 19th century by indentured Indian sugar workers to the Caribbean, and by British traders to Japan. Recipe and ingredients provided by Cookipedia

  • 2 aubergines
  • 2 tablespoons sunflower oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped finely
  • 2 cm root ginger, peeled and julienned
  • 2 tablespoons Thai red curry paste
  • Zest and juice from 1 lime
  • 2 tablespoons muscovado sugar
  • 1 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 200g coconut milk
  1. Trim the aubergines and cut into bite-sized cubes
  2. Heat the oil in a pan or wok and stir-fry the onions, garlic and ginger for 3 or 4 minutes
  3. Add the aubergine and saut√© for 5 minutes or until they start to take some colour
  4. Add the Curry paste and mix well
  5. Pour in 400 ml cold water, add the lime juice and zest, sugar and soy sauce and bring to the boil.
  6. Reduce the heat and simmer for 15 minutes
  7. Add the coconut milk and reheat the curry, being careful not to boil it.
